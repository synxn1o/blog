# Part I: Perturbation Methods for Algebraic Equations

## I. Series Method

### Example 1:  
$$
x^2 + \varepsilon x - 1 = 0
\Rightarrow x = -\frac{1}{2}\varepsilon \pm \sqrt{1 + \frac{1}{4}\varepsilon^2} = x(\varepsilon)
$$

Exact solution:
$$
x = 
\begin{cases}
1 - \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon + \frac{1}{8}\varepsilon^2 - \frac{1}{128}\varepsilon^4 + O(\varepsilon^6) \\
-1 - \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon - \frac{1}{8}\varepsilon^2 + \frac{1}{128}\varepsilon^4 + O(\varepsilon^6)
\end{cases}
$$

---

### (1) Expansion Method

Assume expansion of $x(\varepsilon)$ around $x = 1$:

$$
x(\varepsilon) = 1 + \varepsilon x_1 + \varepsilon^2 x_2 + \cdots
$$

Substitute back into original equation:

$$
x^2 = 1 + 2\varepsilon x_1 + (\varepsilon^2 x_1^2 + 2x_2)\varepsilon^2 + \cdots \\
\varepsilon x = \varepsilon + \varepsilon^2 x_1 + \varepsilon^3 x_2 + \cdots
$$

So the equation becomes:
$$
x^2 + \varepsilon x - 1 = 0
\Rightarrow
\text{(Collect terms by powers of } \varepsilon)
$$

At $\varepsilon = 0$:
$$
O(\varepsilon^0): \quad 1 - 1 = 0 \quad \text{(OK)}
$$

$O(\varepsilon^1)$:
$$
2x_1 - 1 = 0 \Rightarrow x_1 = \frac{1}{2}
$$

$O(\varepsilon^2)$:
$$
x_1^2 + 2x_2 + x_1 = 0 \Rightarrow \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 + 2x_2 + \frac{1}{2} = 0 \Rightarrow x_2 = -\frac{1}{8}
$$

Thus,
$$
x(\varepsilon) = 1 + \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon - \frac{1}{8}\varepsilon^2 + \cdots
$$

> Note: This matches the series expansion up to $\varepsilon^2$. Higher-order terms can be obtained similarly.

---

### (2) Iterative Method (Fixed Point)

Given $f(x) = 0 \Leftrightarrow x = g(x)$

Iterative scheme:
$$
x_{n+1} = g(x_n)
$$

Recall: Converges when $x \in [a,b]$ and $|g'(x)| \leq L < 1$

For our example:
$$
x = \sqrt{1 - \varepsilon x} = g(x)
\Rightarrow
|g'(x)| = \left| \frac{\varepsilon}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \varepsilon x}} \right| < 1
$$

This implies convergence if $x > \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{4}\varepsilon$

Choose initial guess $x_0 = 1$:

- $x_1 = \sqrt{1 - \varepsilon x_0} = \sqrt{1 - \varepsilon} \approx 1 - \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon - \frac{1}{8}\varepsilon^2$
- $x_2 = 1 - \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon + \frac{1}{8}\varepsilon^2 + O(\varepsilon^3)$
- $x_3 = 1 - \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon + \frac{1}{8}\varepsilon^2 - \frac{1}{128}\varepsilon^4 + \cdots$

> Observation: Each iteration adds one more accurate term in the perturbation series.

Compare with exact solution:
$$
\sqrt{1 - \varepsilon} = 1 - \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon - \frac{1}{8}\varepsilon^2 - \frac{1}{16}\varepsilon^3 + \cdots
$$

So:
- $x_1$: accurate up to $\varepsilon$, error at $\varepsilon^2$
- $x_2$: accurate up to $\varepsilon^2$, error at $\varepsilon^3$
- ...

> ✅ **Key Insight**: For each iteration, we gain one more accurate term in the asymptotic expansion.

---

### Example 2: Singular Perturbation

Equation:
$$
\varepsilon^3 x^2 + x - 1 = 0
$$

True solution:
$$
x = -\frac{1}{2\varepsilon} + \frac{\sqrt{1 + 4\varepsilon}}{2\varepsilon}
= 
\begin{cases}
1 - \varepsilon + 2\varepsilon^2 - 5\varepsilon^3 + \cdots \\
-\frac{1}{\varepsilon} - 1 + \varepsilon - 2\varepsilon^2 + 5\varepsilon^3 + \cdots
\end{cases}
$$

At $\varepsilon = 0$: $x = 1$

But note: The second branch has $\mathcal{O}(1/\varepsilon)$ — this is a **singular perturbation** problem.

---

#### (1) Iterative Method Attempt

From:
$$
\varepsilon x^2 + x - 1 = 0 \Rightarrow x^2 = \frac{1 - x}{\varepsilon}
\Rightarrow x = \frac{1}{\varepsilon x} - \frac{1}{\varepsilon} = g(x)
$$

Wait — better form:
$$
x = \frac{1}{\varepsilon x} - \frac{1}{\varepsilon} = g(x)
$$

Then:
$$
|g'(x)| = \left| \frac{1}{\varepsilon} \cdot \left( -\frac{1}{x^2} \right) \right| = \frac{1}{\varepsilon x^2}
\Rightarrow |g'(x)| < 1 \iff |x| > \frac{1}{\sqrt{\varepsilon}}
$$



---

## Summary

| Method               | Applicability                             | Notes                                                                         |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Expansion Method** | Regular perturbations                     | Assumes solution is analytic in $\varepsilon$, expands around known limit     |
| **Iterative Method** | Fixed-point convergence                   | Requires $g'(x) \leq L < 1$, converges slowly but builds accuracy iteratively |
| **Singular Case**    | When $\varepsilon \to 0$ causes breakdown | Standard methods fail; need multiple scales                                   |

---

> 🔍 **Key Takeaway**:  
> - Regular perturbation works well when $x(\varepsilon)$ remains bounded as $\varepsilon \to 0$.  
> - Singular perturbations involve boundary layers or non-uniform behavior — require advanced techniques.  
> - Iterative methods are useful numerically and can recover perturbation series term-by-term.
